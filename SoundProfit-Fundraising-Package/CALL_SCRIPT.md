## Q&A RESPONSES (15 preguntas comunes)

### 1. "Why crypto? It's too volatile."

```
"Great question. Three points:

First, we accept stablecoins like USDC that are 1:1 with the dollar. Zero volatility.

Second, creators can convert to fiat immediately if they want. We just facilitate the transaction.

Third, crypto lets us charge 2% vs 3-5% for credit cards. That difference is millions saved for creators annually.

And if the market demands it, we can add fiat payments in Phase 2. But crypto is our competitive advantage."
```

---

### 2. "What if Beatstars lowers their fees?"

```
"They can't. And here's why:

Beatstars has legacy infrastructure that costs millions to maintain. Payment processors, compliance departments, fund retention systems.

Their operating costs are 15-20% of GMV. If they lower fees to 10%, they operate at a loss.

We have costs of 0.5% of GMV. We can operate profitably at 2%.

It's like when Uber arrived. Taxis couldn't compete not because they didn't want to, but because structurally it was impossible.

Same dynamic here."
```

---

### 3. "How will you acquire users?"

```
"Three proven channels:

Channel 1: Content marketing. Articles like 'Beatstars vs SoundProfit', tutorials for creators. Cost: $0, timeline: 3 months for traction.

Channel 2: Partnerships with DAWs. FL Studio, Ableton have millions of users. We offer 10% revenue share. Direct access to active creators.

Channel 3: Referral program. Creators earn 20% commission for referrals. Viral loop.

Our CAC target is $15. LTV is $36. Ratio 2.4x. Payback in 3 months."
```

---

### 4. "What about regulation?"

```
"We've analyzed this with legal advisors. Three points:

First, we're not an exchange. We don't custody funds. Regulation of exchanges doesn't apply directly.

Second, if needed, we implement KYC/AML. Cost: $5K, timeline: 2 weeks. We have the vendor identified.

Third, we operate globally. If one country regulates heavily, we focus on others. Crypto is global by nature.

And the trend is toward regulatory clarity, not prohibition. Even the SEC is approving Bitcoin ETFs now."
```

```

```
